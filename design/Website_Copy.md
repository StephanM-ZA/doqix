### How Automation Works

**H2:** The Simple Logic Behind Every Automation

Every automation, from the simplest reminder to the most complex multi-system workflow, follows four steps:

**Trigger:** Something happens.
*A new order lands. A form gets submitted. An invoice becomes overdue.*

**Rule:** A decision is made.
*IF the order is over R5,000, THEN flag as high-priority.*

**Condition:** A check runs.
*Is this a returning customer? Has this been paid already?*

**Action:** The task gets done.
*Tag them in the CRM. Send the email. Alert the manager. Generate the PDF.*

That's it. Once it's set up, it runs every time. Perfectly, 24/7, without coffee breaks.

# TONE GUIDE

**The voice:** Smart friend over coffee. You know your stuff and you're excited about it, but you explain it simply because you respect their time.

**Do say:** "Let's fix that." / "We'll tell you straight." / "Your team has better things to do." / "No jargon, we promise."

**Don't say:** "AI-powered." / "Intelligent agents." / "Enterprise-grade." / "Machine learning." / "Leverage synergistic paradigms." / "Innovative solutions." / "Seamless end-to-end digital transformation." / "Empower your team." Every SA competitor leads with AI buzzwords. We lead with understanding. The moment we sound like them, we've lost.

**Rules:**
1. Short sentences beat long ones.
2. Benefits before features. Always answer "So what?"
3. Specific beats vague. "R11,000/month" beats "significant savings."
4. Sound South African. Mention rand, load-shedding, POPIA, WhatsApp. The stuff our people live with.
5. Address the fear. Automation doesn't replace people. It removes the boring stuff from their day.
6. Every section earns its place. If it doesn't move someone toward the CTA, cut it.
