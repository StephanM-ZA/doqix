## Core Value Claim

> **"Your operations run on dozens of manual processes. We'll scope the opportunity and show you the numbers. The ROI speaks for itself."**

At enterprise scale, the ROI conversation shifts:
- Not "hours saved" — it's **FTEs redeployed, error cost avoided, cycle time compressed, risk reduced**
- Not "break-even" — it's **payback within year 1**, 3–5x ROI by year 2

## Illustrative ROI — Mid-Market Enterprise

Company: 80 staff, 6 core systems, ~8 workflows automated (Growth tier).

| Impact | Annual value |
|--------|--------------|
| 3 FTEs' worth of manual ops work redeployed (R400K × 3 loaded) | R1,200,000 |
| Error/rework reduction (20% fewer errors × typical R300K annual cost) | R60,000 |
| Cycle time: quote-to-cash shortened 5 days (cashflow value) | R150,000 |
| Audit/compliance time saved | R80,000 |
| Employee retention impact (1 fewer mid-level hire lost) | R250,000 |
| **Total annual value** | **R1,740,000** |

**Investment (Growth tier):**
- Setup (yr 1 only): R180,000
- Monthly × 12: R420,000
- **Year 1 total: R600,000**

**Year 1 net: R1.14M.**
**Year 2 onward (ongoing): R1.32M/yr net** (no setup).

## The Strategic ROI (narrative, not numbers)

Enterprise buyers care about more than hours. They care about:

1. **Operational resilience** — automation doesn't take sick leave or resign
2. **Scale readiness** — handle 2x volume without 2x headcount
3. **Audit / compliance posture** — consistent, logged, auditable processes
4. **Competitive positioning** — faster cycle times than slower competitors
5. **Talent leverage** — expensive people doing expensive work, not admin
6. **Risk reduction** — fewer manual touchpoints = fewer error-driven losses
7. **Exit / due-diligence value** — operational maturity shows up in valuation

## How to Present ROI in the Proposal

See [`sales_tooling/Proposal_Template.md`](./sales_tooling/Proposal_Template.md) §2 "What We Heard".

**Key structural rule:** Quantify the **cost of inaction** BEFORE presenting the pricing options. Price lands soft when it's compared against a problem they've already agreed is expensive.

Format:

```
Cost of Inaction (per year):
  Time loss:        R_____
  Error/rework:     R_____
  Opportunity cost: R_____
  Total:            R_____  ← this is the anchor

Our Investment:
  Growth tier:      R600K year 1, R420K ongoing

Net value delivered, year 1: R_____
```

## The Outcome-Based Narrative (even if priced as retainer)

Do.Qix prices as retainer + setup. But we **sell using outcome narrative**:

> "You don't pay us for hours. You pay us to make [specific painful thing] stop happening — and to keep it stopped. The retainer is how we stay accountable for that."

This reframes every pricing objection into an outcome conversation:
- "That's a lot" → "Compared to what? The annual cost of not doing it is [X]."
- "Can you discount?" → "We can reduce scope, not price. What matters less?"

## Proof Points to Collect

Once you land enterprise clients:
- FTE-equivalent capacity redeployed (numbered)
- Error rate reduction (before/after, %)
- Cycle-time improvements (days cut from X process)
- Compliance/audit wins (findings resolved, audits passed)
- NPS/satisfaction from stakeholders
- Multi-year contract renewal — the ultimate proof point

These feed:
- Case studies (with permission)
- Reference customer calls
- Sales deck proof sections
