# Enterprise — Target Customer

**Version:** 1.0 · **Date:** 2026-04-15

---

## Who This Is For

- **Organisations with 50+ staff** (often 100–500+)
- **Multi-department operations** running on complex SaaS stacks (or mixed SaaS + legacy)
- **Regulated industries** — financial services, healthcare, legal, insurance
- **Businesses where automation is core infrastructure**, not a side project
- **Companies with explicit compliance requirements** — POPIA, ISO 27001, PCI, sector-specific
- **Ops/IT leaders looking for one accountable partner** instead of a vendor patchwork

### Buyer profile
- **Team size:** 50+ (often far more)
- **Monthly budget for automation:** R15K–R100K+ (setup budget R80K–R500K+)
- **Decision process:** Multi-stakeholder — ops + IT + finance + often legal/compliance
- **Tech comfort:** Internal IT/ops team, mature SaaS adoption, pockets of legacy
- **Pain threshold:** Losing thousands of hours/year across departments; risk from manual errors; audit findings; scaling bottlenecks
- **Buying signal:** "We've outgrown Zapier/Make/internal scripts — we need a real managed partner."

---

## Who This Is NOT For

- Sub-50-staff businesses → **Business** tier
- Teams wanting to self-serve without a discovery sprint → **Team/Business**
- Anyone looking for "just build us this and go away" — we're a managed-service firm
- Buyers unwilling to pay for discovery — it's how we protect our quality and their budget

## Three Customer Archetypes

### 1. The "Outgrown DIY" company
- Was doing automation on Zapier / Make / internal scripts
- Hit the ceiling (volume, reliability, complexity, cost)
- Needs a grown-up managed service
- Typically lands on **Growth**

### 2. The "Regulated & Risky" company
- Financial services, healthcare, insurance, legal
- Has compliance and audit requirements
- Can't afford downtime or data issues
- Typically lands on **Partnership**

### 3. The "Strategic Transformation" company
- Ops leadership running a formal automation / digital programme
- Multiple departments, phased rollout over 12–24 months
- Wants a long-term partner, not a vendor
- Typically starts **Foundation** or **Growth**, grows into **Partnership**

## Red Flags (disqualify or manage carefully)

See [`sales_tooling/Discovery_Questionnaire.md`](./sales_tooling/Discovery_Questionnaire.md) § "Red Flags to Watch For".

Top three:
1. "Send me a quote without discovery" — tire-kicker or price-shopper
2. No named decision-maker — being used as a pricing benchmark
3. Pushback on every commercial term — relationship will be adversarial

## Natural Scale Path Within Enterprise

```
Foundation (R15K/mo, 3 workflows)
  └─ Validates the managed model over 6–12 months
      └─ Upgrade to Growth (R35K/mo, 6–8 workflows + SLA + quarterly reviews)
          └─ Scales further as strategic programme matures
              └─ Upgrade to Partnership (R65K/mo, dedicated engineer, on-call)
```
