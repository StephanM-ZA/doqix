## 1.2 — How We Compare

### How is Do.Qix different from Zapier or Make?
Zapier and Make are DIY tools. You (or your team) build and maintain the automations yourself, pay per task, and learn the platform. Do.Qix is a **managed service**. We do the building, the hosting, the fixing, and the ongoing tweaks. Flat ZAR retainer, no per-task fees, and no team time spent learning a new platform.

### How does Do.Qix compare to hiring a developer or freelancer?
A developer builds and leaves. You're responsible for hosting, monitoring, fixing, and updating. Do.Qix is an ongoing service: we build, host, monitor, fix, and improve your automations every month. No recruitment, no handover risk, no gaps when someone resigns. If your developer leaves tomorrow, your automations stop working. With us, they never do.

### Why not just use my in-house IT team?
Your IT team has a full plate already (network, security, support tickets, infrastructure). Automation is a specialised discipline that requires deep integration knowledge across hundreds of apps. We complement your IT team by owning the automation layer so they can focus on what they're already good at. Many of our Business and Enterprise clients have IT departments. We slot in alongside them.

### How do you compare to offshore automation agencies?
Offshore teams are cheaper per hour, but you pay in other ways: timezone gaps (6-12 hours), communication friction, no local context (they don't know Xero SA vs Xero global, Yoco, Ozow, or how POPIA works), and no accountability when something breaks at 2pm your time. We're in your timezone, we understand SA business realities, and we respond in hours, not days.

### Why not just train my admin team on Zapier/Make?
You can, but here's what happens: they spend weeks learning the platform, build something fragile, then move to a new role or leave. Nobody maintains it. It breaks. Six months later you're back to manual. We've seen this cycle dozens of times. Our model removes that risk entirely: we own the skill, the hosting, and the maintenance. Your team focuses on their actual job.

### What's the difference between Do.Qix and an RPA vendor (UiPath, Automation Anywhere)?
RPA tools automate by mimicking screen clicks. They're designed for enterprises with legacy desktop applications. They cost R50,000+/month, need dedicated infrastructure, and break when a button moves. We use API-based integration (n8n), which is faster, more reliable, and a fraction of the cost. For SMEs running cloud tools, RPA is overkill. We're the right size.

### Why use Do.Qix instead of self-hosting n8n myself?
n8n is open-source. You can self-host it for free. But you'll need: a server, DevOps knowledge, monitoring, backups, security hardening, SSL, updates, and someone to build and maintain the workflows. Our clients pay us so they don't have to think about any of that. You get the power of n8n without the ops burden.

Yes. We treat your data as restricted, minimise data handling to what's needed for the workflow, maintain audit logs, and don't share client data with third parties. ### What is POPIA and how does it affect my business automations?
POPIA (Protection of Personal Information Act) governs how you collect, store, and process personal data in South Africa. Any automation handling customer data (names, emails, payment info) must comply. We build POPIA compliance into every workflow: data minimisation, access controls, audit trails, and SA-hosted infrastructure. You stay compliant without thinking about it.

## 1.10 — SEO-Targeted Questions

### What is workflow automation and how does it work?
Workflow automation connects your business tools so they work together without manual effort. When something happens in one system (a new order, a form submission, a payment), it automatically triggers actions in other systems (create an invoice, send a notification, update a spreadsheet). No human clicks required.

### What is n8n and why should I care?
n8n is an open-source workflow automation platform. It's what we use under the hood. You don't need to know or care about it. What matters to you: it connects 400+ apps, it's not usage-based (no per-task fees), it's self-hostable (your data stays where you want it), and it's transparent (no vendor lock-in). We handle all the technical complexity.

### What is the difference between RPA and workflow automation?
RPA (Robotic Process Automation) mimics human clicks on a screen. It's designed for legacy systems with no APIs. Workflow automation connects systems via APIs (data pipes between tools). For most SA SMEs running cloud software, workflow automation is faster, cheaper, and more reliable than RPA. We use workflow automation.

### How long does it take to see results from automation?
Most clients see their first workflow live within 1-2 weeks. Time savings are immediate once it's running. Typical feedback: "I forgot I used to do that manually" within the first month.

## 2.1 — Technical Details

### What is your uptime guarantee?
We target 99.5% uptime on our hosted infrastructure. This is monitored 24/7 with automated alerts. If a workflow fails, we're notified immediately and begin resolution. Enterprise Partnership clients receive a formal SLA with uptime commitments and service credits.

### What happens if an API we depend on goes down?
We build all workflows with error handling and retry logic. If an external API (like Xero or Google) has an outage, your workflow retries automatically when the service recovers. No data is lost. For critical workflows, we can add fallback paths and escalation alerts.

### How do you handle API rate limits?
We design workflows to respect rate limits from day one. For high-volume processes, we implement queuing, batching, and throttling to stay within limits. If a service has restrictive limits, we discuss this during scoping and design accordingly.

### Is there a limit on how many times a workflow can run?
No. Unlike Zapier or Make, we don't charge per task or per execution. Your workflows run as often as needed. Whether it triggers 10 times a day or 10,000, the price is the same.

### How do you handle version control and rollbacks?
Every workflow change is versioned. If an update causes issues, we roll back to the previous working version within minutes. Enterprise clients get formal change management with approval workflows before any production change goes live.

### Can my workflows handle high volume (thousands of records per day)?
Yes. n8n handles high-throughput workloads well. We've built workflows processing tens of thousands of records daily. For extreme volumes, we design with batching, parallel processing, and dedicated infrastructure.

### Do you support webhook-based real-time triggers?
Yes. Most of our workflows are event-driven (webhooks), meaning they execute instantly when something happens (new order, form submission, payment received). We also support scheduled triggers (run every hour, daily at 6am, etc.) for batch processes.

### Can you integrate with legacy or on-premise systems?
Yes, on the **Enterprise** tier. Think SAP, Oracle, Syspro, Sage 200/300, custom warehouse or point-of-sale systems, file-based integrations (CSV, SFTP), or on-prem databases.

When you've used all your workflow slots and want more, when you need faster support response, or when your needs span multiple departments. We'll often suggest it proactively when we see you hitting the ceiling.

## 2.7 — Migration and Transition

### I'm currently on Zapier/Make. How does migration work?
We audit your current setup, identify what to keep, what to improve, and what to retire. Then we rebuild on our hosted n8n (usually improving the workflows in the process). Typical migration takes 2-4 weeks depending on complexity. Your existing automations keep running until the new ones are tested and live.

### I have automations built by a previous developer/agency. Can you take over?
Yes. We audit what exists, document it, identify what's working and what's fragile, then take ownership. If it's on n8n already, it's straightforward. If it's on another platform, we migrate. Either way, you get a clean, documented, monitored setup.

### Can you migrate from custom-built integrations (Python scripts, cron jobs)?
Yes. We regularly replace fragile custom scripts with robust, monitored n8n workflows. The result is the same logic, but with proper error handling, monitoring, alerting, and a UI your team can actually understand.

### What if I want to leave Do.Qix and go in-house?
We support that fully. You own everything. We provide complete handover: workflow definitions, documentation, credential inventory, architecture diagrams, and a transition period where your internal team can ask questions.

## 2.8 — AI and Advanced Capabilities

### What kinds of AI can you integrate into workflows?
Document summarisation, email classification, data extraction from PDFs/images, sentiment analysis, content drafting, lead scoring, and intelligent routing. We use whichever model fits the task (OpenAI, Anthropic, local models). AI costs are passed through transparently at actual usage.

### Can AI make decisions in my workflows?
Yes, with guardrails. AI can classify, score, and route. For high-stakes decisions (approving payments, sending legal documents), we always include human-in-the-loop checkpoints. AI handles the 80% that's routine; humans handle the 20% that needs judgment.

### How much does AI integration cost on top of my plan?
AI model usage is billed at actual cost (typically R0.01-R0.50 per workflow execution depending on the task). We don't mark up AI costs. The integration work itself is either included in your retainer (simple classification/extraction) or quoted as custom development (complex multi-step AI pipelines).

### Can you build me a customer-facing chatbot?
Yes, under custom development. We build chatbots that integrate with your knowledge base, CRM, and booking systems. Usually Enterprise scope because of the complexity and compliance requirements of customer-facing AI.

### Can you migrate me off Zapier / Make / existing automations?
Yes. We audit your current setup, re-platform to our hosted n8n, and take over running it. Often cheaper than staying on per-task pricing once you've got more than a handful of workflows.

### Do you build custom dashboards / BI reports?
Yes, as custom development (quoted separately). Not included in standard retainer. The retainer is for workflows, not reporting tools.

## 2.9 — Governance and Reporting

### Do you provide reports on what my automations are doing?
Yes. Business clients get a monthly strategy call that includes performance data. Enterprise clients get formal reporting (execution counts, success rates, time saved, issues resolved). Solo and Team clients can request a summary anytime.

Request it anytime via email. We'll send you the full workflow definition and a plain-English description of what each automation does, its triggers, and its outputs. You own this documentation.

Yes. We can provide read-only documentation or, for Enterprise clients, view-only access to the automation platform. Let us know what they need and we'll arrange appropriate access.
