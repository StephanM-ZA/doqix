**Version:** 2.0
**Date:** February 11, 2026
**Aligned with:** Website_Copy.md v7.0, Social_Media_Plan.md v1.1

## 1. Core Principles

This SEO strategy is designed to be simple, effective, and sustainable for a small business. The core principles are:

*   **Focus on a Niche:** We are not trying to compete with global giants like Zapier on broad terms. We are targeting SMEs in South Africa who are looking for a "done-for-you" automation service.
*   **Be the Local Expert:** The strategy is heavily focused on Local SEO and establishing Do.Qix as the go-to expert for automation in South Africa.
*   **Content is King:** High-quality, helpful content is the foundation of this strategy. We will create content that answers our target audience's questions and solves their problems.
*   **Mobile-First:** The website will be designed and optimized for mobile devices first, as this is how most users will find it.
*   **Anti-Buzzword Principle:** All SEO content follows the same rule as the website — we lead with understanding, not technology. No "AI-powered," "intelligent agents," "enterprise-grade," or "machine learning" in any content we publish. We rank for what people search, but we explain it in plain language.

## 3. On-Page SEO

*   **Titles and Meta Descriptions:** Every page will have a unique, compelling title and meta description that includes the primary keyword for that page, clearly communicating the benefit of solving the "Productivity Trap." See Website_Copy.md v7.0 for approved meta descriptions and page titles.
*   **Headings:** Headings (H1, H2, H3) will be used to structure the content and will include relevant keywords, addressing concepts like "The Problem," "How It Works," "What We Automate," and "Real Results."
*   **Content:** The content will be well-written, engaging, and easy to read. It will be optimized for the target keywords, but not at the expense of readability. We will explain terms like "RPA" and "BPA" in accessible, jargon-free language — consistent with our anti-buzzword principle.
*   **Images:** All images will have descriptive alt text, optimizing for keywords like "workflow automation diagram" or "automated invoice process."

## 4. Content Marketing Strategy

The goal of our content marketing is to attract, educate, and convert our target audience by providing solutions to their "Productivity Trap" challenges.

*   **Blog:** We will maintain a regular blog with posts that are relevant to our target audience.
    *   **Pillar Content:** We will create a few comprehensive "pillar" posts on topics like "The Ultimate Guide to Business Automation for SMEs in South Africa," incorporating the **"5-Step Plan" for getting started**.
    *   **Supporting Posts:** We will create shorter posts on more specific topics, such as "5 Repetitive Tasks You Can Automate Today" or "How to Choose the Right Automation Tool for Your Business." We can also have posts like "Understanding RPA vs. BPA: What's Right for Your Business?" and "Calculating Your Automation ROI: A Simple Guide."
*   **Educational Content:** We will repurpose the existing text files ("5 Surprising Truths," BPA Implementation Blueprint, etc.) into blog posts, social media updates, and possibly a downloadable e-book. We can also create a series explaining the **"Anatomy of a Workflow"** and its components (Trigger, Rule, Condition, Action).
*   **Case Studies:** We will create detailed case studies based on our SA client scenarios — the Joburg property agency (lead response time), Cape Town accounting firm (Friday reports automated), e-commerce brand (no extra hire needed), and Durban consultancy (onboarding from 2 days to 20 minutes). Real client names and numbers will replace these scenarios as they come in.
*   **Video:** We will use the video files to create short, engaging videos for the website and social media. The "Intro to Workflow Automation" video is perfect for the home page. We can create animated videos explaining concepts like BPA and ROI visually.
*   **Social Media Content:** Blog content should be coordinated with the Social_Media_Plan.md. The 5 content pillars (Problem, Fix, Proof, People, Offer) guide what we write and when. SEO blog posts feed directly into social repurposing.

## 5. Local SEO Strategy

*   **Google Business Profile:** We will create and optimize a Google Business Profile for Do.Qix. This will include:
    *   Consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone Number) across all online directories.
    *   High-quality photos and videos.
    *   Regular posts with updates and offers.
    *   Encouraging and responding to customer reviews.
*   **Local Keyword Targeting:** Rather than creating separate landing pages (the site uses a 3-page architecture: Home, Services, Contact), we will target local keywords through blog content, Google Business Profile posts, and schema markup. Blog posts can be city-targeted (e.g., "Workflow Automation for Johannesburg Businesses") without adding pages to the main site nav.
*   **Local Link Building:** We will seek out opportunities to get links from other local businesses and directories.

## 6. Technical SEO

*   **Site Speed:** The website will be optimized for speed to ensure a good user experience. Target: under 3 seconds load time.
*   **Mobile-Friendliness:** The website will be fully responsive and mobile-friendly.
*   **Schema Markup:** We will use schema markup to help Google understand the content of our website. This will include schema for "Local Business," "Service," and "Article."
*   **SSL Certificate:** The website will have an SSL certificate to ensure it is secure.

## 7. Off-Page SEO

*   **Link Building:** We will focus on building high-quality backlinks from relevant and authoritative websites. This will include guest posting on relevant blogs and participating in online forums.
*   **Social Media:** LinkedIn is the primary platform (see Social_Media_Plan.md). Facebook secondary, Instagram supporting. Content shared on social drives traffic back to the site and builds domain authority signals.
