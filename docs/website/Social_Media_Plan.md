## 1. Strategy Overview

### 1.1. What Social Media Needs to Do

Social media isn't the sales channel — the website and free automation plan call are. Social media does three jobs:

1. **Awareness** — Put Do.Qix in front of SA business owners who don't know they need automation yet
2. **Trust** — Show we understand their world (not that we know fancy tech words)
3. **Pipeline** — Drive warm leads to the website / free automation plan CTA

### 1.2. Revenue-Linked Targets

Based on the 6-month MRR projection (Pricing_Strategy.md):

| Month | New clients needed | Social media's job |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 3 | Generate 10-15 enquiries → convert 3 |
| 2 | 3 | Generate 15-20 enquiries → convert 3 |
| 3 | 3 | Generate 20-25 enquiries → convert 3 |

Assuming a 20-25% conversion rate from enquiry to client, social media needs to drive **10-25 warm leads per month** to the website or WhatsApp.

### 1.3. Voice Rules (From Tone Guide)

- Smart friend over coffee — not a tech company selling you something
- No "AI-powered," "intelligent agents," "enterprise-grade," "machine learning"
- Specific beats vague — use rand amounts, hours saved, real examples
- Sound South African — rand, load-shedding, POPIA, WhatsApp, the reality of running a business here
- Address the fear — automation doesn't replace people, it removes the boring stuff

## 2. Platform Strategy

### 2.1. Primary: LinkedIn

**Why:** This is where SA business owners, ops managers, and decision-makers live. It's where business buying decisions start. Organic reach is still strong compared to other platforms.

**Posting cadence:** 4x per week (Mon, Tue, Thu, Fri)
**Content split:** 40% educational, 30% proof/results, 20% personality/behind-the-scenes, 10% direct CTA

### 2.2. Secondary: Facebook

**Why:** SA has massive Facebook adoption, especially among SME owners in property, services, retail. Many SA business groups are active here. Lower effort — repurpose LinkedIn content with slight adjustments.

**Posting cadence:** 3x per week (Mon, Wed, Fri)
**Content split:** Same as LinkedIn, slightly more casual tone

### 2.3. Supporting: Instagram

**Why:** Visual proof of work — before/after workflow screenshots, carousel explainers, short Reels. Good for brand personality. Lower priority than LinkedIn/Facebook for lead gen.

**Posting cadence:** 2-3x per week
**Content type:** Carousels, Reels (under 60 sec), visual stats

### 2.4. Optional: X (Twitter)

**Why:** Quick takes, thought leadership, engagement with SA business community. Low effort, high visibility if content hits.

**Posting cadence:** 3-5x per week (short takes, repurposed from LinkedIn)

### 2.5. Not Now

- TikTok — audience skews too young for our buyer
- YouTube — save for later when we have case study videos and the intro video ready

## 3. Content Pillars

Every post falls into one of 5 pillars. This keeps content varied but always on-brand.

### Pillar 1: "The Problem" (Awareness)
Show the pain of manual work. Make people feel seen.
- The 30% stat (a full day lost every week)
- "You know the feeling" scenarios (copy-pasting, Friday report marathons, chasing invoices)
- Cost of manual errors in rands
- The Productivity Trap concept

### Pillar 2: "The Fix" (Education)
Teach how automation works in plain language. No jargon.
- How a workflow thinks (Trigger → Rule → Condition → Action)
- The 5-Step Plan (Identify → Map → Start Small → Test → Monitor)
- Quick win examples (invoicing, lead routing, onboarding)
- "Did you know?" facts about automation

### Pillar 3: "The Proof" (Trust)
Show real results. Specific numbers. SA context.
- Case study snapshots (property agency, accounting firm, e-commerce brand, consultancy)
- Before/after comparisons
- Hours saved, errors eliminated, money recovered
- Client quotes (once available — use scenario-based results until then)

### Pillar 4: "The People" (Personality)
Show the human behind Do.Qix. Build connection.
- Behind-the-scenes of building workflows
- "A day in the life" of an automation builder
- Opinions on business in SA (load-shedding, rand, POPIA realities)
- Celebrating client wins
- The story of why Do.Qix exists

### Pillar 5: "The Offer" (Conversion)
Direct CTA posts. Use sparingly — earn the right first.
- Free automation plan offer
- ROI calculator (once live)
- Pricing transparency ("here's what it costs — in rands")
- Launch offer: first month at 50% on any plan
- Training included for Business/Enterprise tiers
- Enterprise CTA: "Show Me What's Possible"

## 13. Blueprint-Sourced Content Bank

*Drawn from "An Implementation Blueprint for Business Process Automation" (planning docs). These are deeper-cut posts that position Do.Qix as the expert who actually understands the discipline — not just the tools.*

### Post Bank — Ready to Slot Into Any Week

**BP-1 | Pillar 2: The Fix | "Why most automation projects fail"**
> Most automation projects don't fail because of the technology.
>
> They fail because someone skipped the planning.
>
> They automated a broken process. They didn't talk to the people doing the actual work. They picked the wrong task to start with. They rushed to "go live" without testing the edge cases.
>
> That's why our process starts with mapping — not building. We look at how things work today, find where it breaks, fix that first, and only then automate.

**BP-2 | Pillar 2: The Fix | "Optimise before you automate"**
> Here's something most automation agencies won't tell you:
>
> Sometimes the answer isn't automation. It's a better process.
>

> We strip out the waste first. Simplify the steps. Standardise the handoffs. Then — and only then — we automate what's left.
>
> That's the difference between doing things faster and doing things better.
>
> (Or as the old saying goes: "Automation applied to an inefficient operation will magnify the inefficiency.")
