# Do.Qix Website Site Map

**Version:** 3.0
**Date:** February 11, 2026
**Aligned with:** Website_Copy.md v7.0
**Purpose:** Define the navigational hierarchy for Do.Qix. Dead simple — three pages, flat nav, one CTA. Every click either deepens understanding or moves toward conversion. No dead ends. No confusion.

---

## Site Map

Three pages. Flat hierarchy. No sub-menus.

| Page | URL | Purpose | User thinks... |
|------|-----|---------|----------------|
| **Home** | `/` | Introduce the problem, the solution, and why us. Drive to Services or Contact. | "I get it. This is for me." |
| **Services** | `/services` | Show exactly what we automate, how we work, and what it's worth. Build confidence. | "They can actually do this. I want in." |
| **Contact** | `/contact` | Capture the lead. Make it dead simple to reach out. | "That was easy. Let's talk." |

### Supporting Pages (Not in main nav)

| Page | URL | Purpose |
|------|-----|---------|
| **Thank You** | `/thank-you` | Post-form confirmation. Reinforce next steps. Link to content. |
| **Privacy Policy** | `/privacy` | POPIA compliance. Footer link only. |
| **404** | `/404` | Friendly redirect. Links to Home and Contact. |

## Navigation

**Type:** Sticky header. Fixed on scroll. Desktop and mobile.

**Nav links:** Home | Services | Contact
- No sub-menus. No dropdowns. No hamburger menus on desktop.
- Mobile: Hamburger with the same 3 links + CTA button.

**CTA button (always visible in header):** "Start Free"
- Background: --action (#0886B5)
- Text: #FFFFFF
- Links to: `/contact`

**Logo:** "Do.Qix" with "Efficiency, Engineered" tagline lockup
- Links to: `/`

## User Journey — The Straight Line

The site is designed as a straight line from "What is this?" to "I need this."

```
HOME (Aha!) → SERVICES (How?) → CONTACT (Yes!)
```

**Every page has:**
1. One primary CTA: "Start Free" → links to `/contact`
2. Cross-links to the next logical page
3. No dead ends — every section leads somewhere

**Cross-linking strategy:**
- Home hero CTA → `/contact`
- Home "How It Works" CTA → `/contact`
- Home bottom CTA → `/contact`
- Services CTA (multiple) → `/contact`
- Services "Quick Wins" → `/contact`
- Contact → self (form submission → `/thank-you`)
- Thank You → Home or blog content (when available)

## Anti-Drop-Off Rules

1. **Maximum 3 clicks to CTA from any page.** In practice, the CTA is visible on every page in the header.
2. **No orphan pages.** Every page links forward and backward.
3. **No separate Pricing page.** Pricing info lives on Services — one less page to bounce from.
4. **No separate About page.** "Why Us" lives on Home — keeps the journey moving.
5. **No blog in nav** (yet). Blog posts will exist but are reached via footer/search, not primary nav. They shouldn't pull people out of the conversion flow.
6. **Exit-intent popup** on all pages catches bouncing visitors with one final CTA.
