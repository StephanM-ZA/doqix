## 1. Pricing Philosophy

Do.Qix prices on **value delivered**, not headcount. Our clients pay for workflows that save them time and money — not for the number of people who happen to benefit.

**Core principles:**
- Flat, predictable monthly retainer — no per-user, per-task, or credit-based billing
- Everything in South African rands — no dollar-denominated surprises
- Low barrier to entry — even a solopreneur can say yes today
- Month-to-month on every tier — we keep clients because we deliver, not because of contracts
- Every tier includes hosting, monitoring, and support — no nickel-and-diming

**Business strategy:** Retainable monthly recurring revenue (MRR). Get clients in the door easily, deliver obvious value, and let the retention do the heavy lifting. Upsells happen naturally when one workflow proves itself.

---

## 2. Why We Moved Away From Per-User Pricing

The original v5.0 pricing used a per-user model (R99/R79/R69 per user). This was changed because:

1. **Automation isn't per-user.** A workflow runs whether 2 people benefit or 50. Per-user is a SaaS model (Slack, Office 365), not an automation service model.
2. **It punishes growth.** A 30-person company pays more than a 10-person company for the same workflows. This contradicts our own messaging.
3. **Tiers fought each other.** The per-user math made higher tiers cheaper than lower ones at certain team sizes.
4. **Build fee ranges created uncertainty.** "R4,000 - R7,000" gives customers exactly the surprise our copy promises to eliminate.

---

## 3. Why We Dropped Heavy Build Fees

The v1.0 pricing strategy proposed R3,500-R5,000 per workflow as a build fee. This was reconsidered because:

1. **It blocks the smallest clients.** A solopreneur with R1,000/mo tool budget won't pay R5,000 upfront on faith. They haven't experienced the value yet.
2. **The strategy is MRR, not project revenue.** Heavy build fees are a project-based model. Our goal is recurring retainers. The monthly should do the work.
3. **Our actual build cost is manageable.** A workflow takes 4-8 hours to build. At R999+/mo, we recoup that investment within 2-3 months of a retained client — even without a build fee.
4. **Month-to-month commitment means the value has to speak for itself.** If it does, clients stay. If it doesn't, no build fee would have saved us anyway.

### 4.2. Tier Design Logic

**Solo (R999/mo) — The door opener.**
- Zero upfront cost. A solopreneur can say yes today.
- One workflow is enough to prove the value. "Your invoicing runs itself now."
- Break-even for Do.Qix: month 2-3. Profitable from month 3+.
- Natural upsell: "That's saving me 8 hours a month. Can you automate my lead follow-ups too?" → moves to Team.

**Team (R2,500/mo) — The sweet spot.**
- Small setup fee (R1,500) filters tyre-kickers but isn't scary.
- 3 workflows covers the core pain for most small teams (e.g., invoicing + lead management + reporting).
- This is where most clients will land after proving value on Solo.
- Highlighted as "Most popular" to anchor decision-making.

**Business (R5,500/mo) — Real operational change.**
- 6 workflows = serious process overhaul. Finance, sales, ops, support — multiple departments benefit.
- R2,500 setup is justified because scoping 6 workflows takes more upfront time.
- Monthly strategy call builds the long-term relationship and surfaces new automation opportunities.
- Clients here are committed and unlikely to churn.

**Enterprise (Custom quote) — The ceiling.**
- Signals we can handle bigger, more complex work without capping ourselves at R5,500.
- Captures 50+ person companies that need custom scoping, multi-department rollouts, or integrations with legacy systems.
- "Request a quote" is standard at this level — these deals are relationship-sold, not self-serve.
- Makes Solo/Team/Business feel accessible by contrast.
- No published price means we can price based on the specific value delivered.

## 5. The Upsell Path

The pricing is designed so clients naturally graduate upward:

```
Solo (R999/mo)
  → "This is saving me hours. Can you automate [second thing]?"
  → Team (R2,500/mo)
    → "My team needs this across departments."
    → Business (R5,500/mo)
      → "We want to automate everything."
      → Enterprise (custom)
```

**Key insight:** The hardest sale is the first one. Once someone is on Solo and sees a workflow running, the upgrade conversation is easy — they're selling themselves.
