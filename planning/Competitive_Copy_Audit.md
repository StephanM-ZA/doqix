## 2. Where Our Copy WINS (Keep These)

These are unique to Do.Qix and no competitor touches them:

1. **"Stop the Busywork. Start What Matters."** — Best hero headline in the SA market. Short, punchy, benefit-driven. Nobody else has this clarity.

2. **"We fix the process first, then automate it"** + Bill Gates quote — Intellectually honest. No competitor positions this way. Most promise fast results; we promise *correct* results.

3. **"We price in rands, not dollars"** — Direct, competitive, relevant. Nobody else says this.

4. **Rand-denominated ROI examples** (R11,000/month, R30,000/month, R20,000/month) — No competitor quantifies in ZAR.

5. **Load-shedding, POPIA, WhatsApp** — Authentically South African. Only Concise Technologies and Daisy Solutions touch on these, and weakly.

6. **"If automation doesn't make sense for you, we'll say so"** — Radical honesty. Nobody else makes this promise.

7. **"What you stop doing"** under each automation type — Practical, relatable. Nobody else structures their services page this way.

8. **"This page got automated out of existence"** (404) — Fun, memorable.

9. **Job-loss anxiety addressed directly** — "We're not replacing anyone." Only BrightSync touches this. We do it better.

10. **Full funnel thinking** — Thank you page, auto-response email, cookie banner. No SA competitor has this level of thought-through copy.

11. **5-Step Plan** — More structured and trustworthy than competitors' vague "our process" sections.

12. **FAQ section on Contact page** — Reduces friction. No competitor does this well.

## 4. What's MISSING From Our Copy (2026 Gaps)

### A. The AI Angle
**Problem:** In 2026, Zapier's hero is "Transformative AI for every team." AgentK leads with "smart AI solutions." n8n positions as "AI workflow automation tool." Our copy barely mentions AI.

**Risk:** We look behind the curve. AI is the headline for every competitor in 2026.

**Fix:** We don't need to rebrand as AI-first, but we need to acknowledge it. Our angle: "AI that works for you — not the other way around." We use AI inside our automations (smart routing, document processing, content generation) but we don't make the client learn AI. That's our differentiator.

### B. Industry-Specific Use Cases
**Problem:** Every SA competitor speaks generically. Nobody owns specific verticals (legal, healthcare, property, retail, logistics).

**Opportunity:** Even mentioning 2-3 SA industries specifically would set us apart.

### C. Speed/Timeline Specifics
**Problem:** AgentK says "results in 2-4 weeks." Agile Automate says "90 days ROI." We say "most clients reclaim 8-15 hours within the first month" — this is good but we should be bolder on delivery timelines.

### D. Named SA Case Studies
**Problem:** We use international examples (NHS, Bordr, Doe). Great for credibility but no SA-specific stories yet.

**Fix:** Flag placeholder spots for SA case studies. When real ones exist, they'll be our biggest competitive advantage.

## 5. Recommended Copy Updates

### HIGH IMPACT — Do These

1. **Replace the generic benefit triad** ("Save Time, Reduce Costs, Eliminate Errors") with something that sounds like us, not like a brochure:
   - Option A: "Your mornings back. Your budgets intact. Your data clean."
   - Option B: "Less admin. Fewer mistakes. More of the work you actually signed up for."
   - Option C: "Reclaim the hours. Cut the errors. Do the work that matters."

2. **Add a light AI mention** to the Hero or "What We Do" section:
   - "We build smart, AI-powered automations that handle the work your team shouldn't be doing."
   - Keep it casual — we're not an AI company, we're an automation company that uses AI where it makes sense.

3. **Replace "repetitive tasks" in at least half the instances** with more vivid language:
   - "the same 47 steps every Monday morning"
   - "the spreadsheet shuffle"
   - "the inbox treadmill"
   - "the copy-paste marathon"
   - "the admin hamster wheel"

4. **Make "No tech skills needed" more distinctive:**
   - Current: "No software to learn. No tech skills needed."
   - Better: "You won't touch a single line of code. You won't learn a new dashboard. You won't sit through a training session. We do all of it."

5. **Add delivery timeline specifics:**
   - "Simple workflows go live in days. Complex multi-system builds take 2-4 weeks. We'll give you an exact timeline before we start."

6. **Replace "Trusted by South African businesses" with something specific:**
   - "100+ workflows running for SA businesses right now"
   - Or, once available: "[X] hours saved for SA teams this month"

### MEDIUM IMPACT — Nice to Have

7. **Add 2-3 industry mentions** to the Services page:
   - "Whether you're in property, professional services, e-commerce, healthcare, or logistics — if there's a manual process eating your time, we can automate it."

8. **Add a "Not Sure?" micro-section** between automation examples:
   - "Not sure if your process qualifies? That's what the free plan call is for. We'll tell you straight — even if the answer is 'don't automate this yet.'"

9. **Strengthen the "Why Not Zapier/Make" angle** in Why Us:
   - "Your competitors are on Zapier watching their dollar bills climb every month. Or stuck on Make trying to decode credit-based pricing. You'll know your number — in rands — before we start."

10. **Add a "The Do.Qix Difference" one-liner** that captures our philosophy in a sentence:
    - "We don't just automate. We optimize first, automate second, and stay with you after."

## 7. Verdict

**Our copy is already the strongest in the SA market.** The hero, the Bill Gates angle, the rand pricing, the honesty positioning, the SA-authentic voice, and the structured 5-step plan are all genuine differentiators.

**But it has 2026 blind spots:**
- AI needs a mention (not a rebrand — just an acknowledgement)
- Some sections still read like automation copywriting templates (the benefit triad, "repetitive tasks" overuse)
- A few phrases are shared with AgentK almost word-for-word

**Recommended next step:** Apply the HIGH IMPACT changes (items 1-6) to the existing copy. This will sharpen what's already good and eliminate the overlap.
