## How Automation Thinks
Automation follows a simple, logical sequence—the "universal language" of workflows:

1.  **Trigger:** An event occurs (e.g., a new order, a form submission).
2.  **Rule:** A simple "if-then" statement is fired.
3.  **Condition:** A check is performed (e.g., "Is the order over $500?").
4.  **Action:** A task is executed (e.g., tagging a customer as a VIP in the CRM).

### BPA vs. RPA
| Term | Meaning | Scope |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RPA** | Robotic Process Automation | A digital assistant mimicking a single human task (e.g., moving data). |
| **BPA** | Business Process Automation | Redesigning an entire workflow and connecting multiple systems. |

## Anatomy of a Workflow: Case Study (Bordr)
**Bordr** helps people relocate to Portugal. Their bottleneck was getting clients a Portuguese tax ID—a bureaucratic nightmare. They built a **digital assembly line**:

* **Trigger:** Customer fills out a form.
* **Action 1:** Data sent to Airtable database.
* **Action 2:** Legal PDF generated automatically.
* **Action 3:** Personalized emails sent to the customer.
* **Action 4:** Partner law firm alerted with specific instructions.

**Result:** This automation turned a side project into a **six-figure business** by making their business model possible.

## Getting Started: A 5-Step Plan
1.  **Identify:** Find a task that is repetitive, rule-based, and frequent.
2.  **Map:** Draw the process as it exists today. Identify where it breaks.
3.  **Start Small:** Pick one manageable process for a quick win.
4.  **Test & Refine:** Try to break it. Ensure **idempotency** (can it run twice without errors?).
5.  **Monitor:** Track success rates and time saved.

### Quick Win Ideas
* **Sales:** Move web leads directly into your CRM.
* **Support:** Automatically tag and sort incoming tickets.
* **E-commerce:** Set up back-in-stock notifications.
